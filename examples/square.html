Year 2048

The climate changed. The Mediterranean died. Europe walled itself off.

Humanity scattered across a fractured solar system — but brought every war, grudge, and power grab along for the ride.

Gravity Well

Earth, Luna, orbitals. Dense, surveilled, corporate-controlled.

The Void

Mars, Belt, outer stations. Sparse, lawless, opportunity.

Deniable Asset

You are a mercenary operator. Jobs too dirty, too dangerous, or too illegal for corporate militaries.

  • No levels, no classes — capability is bought, spliced, or installed
  • Your identity is your loadout: weapons, augments, contacts, debts
  • Every upgrade has a cost. Every job leaves a mark.
  • The crew is your safety net — and your complication

Action Pool

Build a pool of 2–5 d20s

Split dice across action axes. All resolve simultaneously — every die does something.

Natural 20 succeeds and grants a bonus die. Chain indefinitely.

All-Fail

Every die misses? Catastrophic failure. The GM gets a complication.

Harm & Consequences

Three harm slots. Fill them all and you're ousted — out of the scene, not dead (unless fiction demands it).

Gear State

  • Transient — lost this scene, recoverable
  • Permanent — damaged, needs repair
  • Broken — gone until replaced

Three Principles

Optionality

Every situation has multiple valid approaches. The system never funnels you into a single correct play.

Equality

NPCs use the same rules as players. If you can do it, so can they. No GM fiat dice.

Specificity

Mechanics care about the fiction. A monofilament blade and a breaching charge solve different problems differently.

Why FREE//FALL?

  • Simultaneous resolution — no initiative, no waiting for your turn
  • Gear over levels — your loadout is your character sheet
  • Crew-first — solo play works, but the game sings with a crew
  • Fiction-weight — light rules, heavy narrative consequences
  • Open license — CC-BY / MIT, hack it, fork it, ship it
  • Built for one-shots — session zero in 15 minutes
